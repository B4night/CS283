In 1973, the Paris Peace Accords were signed, officially ending the United States' involvement in the war. However, the Accords did not bring an end to the conflict, as the North Vietnamese Army continued to push south, capturing key cities and territories.

On April 29, 1975, the North Vietnamese Army launched a final assault on Saigon, and by the next morning, they had captured the city. President Thieu fled the country, and the South Vietnamese government collapsed.

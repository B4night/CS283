August 6, 1945, was a day that would be etched in the memories of humanity forever. It was the day when the United States dropped an atomic bomb on the Japanese city of Hiroshima, killing an estimated 70,000 to 80,000 people instantly. The bomb, known as "Little Boy," was dropped from a B-29 bomber called the Enola Gay, and it exploded with a force equivalent to 15,000 tons of TNT.

The city of Hiroshima was a bustling metropolis, home to over 340,000 people. It was a major industrial and commercial center, and the Japanese military had a significant presence there.

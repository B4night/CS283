Once upon a time, in the late 17th and 18th centuries, a group of determined individuals set out to create a new nation. Their journey was marked by struggle, vision, and the birth of a new political experiment that would shape the world.

In 1620, the Mayflower arrived in Plymouth Harbor, carrying English pilgrims seeking to escape the religious persecution they faced at home. They established Plymouth Colony, governed by their own laws, a blend of biblical principles and practical governance. While they faced harsh conditions and conflicts with the local Native American communities, the colony survived, laying the groundwork for future developments.

By the mid-18th century, the American colonies had grown into thriving societies with their own economies, cultures, and governance systems. However, tensions with Britain boiled over due to issues like taxation without representation and the desire for greater political freedom.

The Cuban Missile Crisis of October 1962 was a pivotal and terrifying moment in Cold War history, a time when the world stood on the brink of nuclear annihilation. It all began when Soviet leader Nikita Khrushchev secretly deployed intermediate-range ballistic missiles (IRBMs) in Cuba, just 90 miles away from the U.S. mainland. This move was seen as a direct challenge to U.S. sovereignty and a display of Soviet strength, especially during a time of heightened tensions between the two superpowers.
